silver's thoughts on the web

so, about the new web...

i've seen all of those internet manifestos about how people want the old web back, and i want to present my own take on it.
i *love* sites with passion put into them. i mean, i've always put so much heart-bursting passion into my own personal projects.
but i dont think what most 'take back the web' people realize is, well, taking back the web isnt that easy.
search engines are made for popular sites and algorithims. passionate sites, oozing the fact they were made
by a *human* for a *human*, wont show up that easily.
even then, do you think all of the people outside want to 'take back the web'?
what this results in is small communities that kinda just talk about taking back the web while nothing really happens.

what these people also dont realize is that, well...i *love* graphic design, so i may be biased, but:
you cant stick with web 1.0 sites all of the time.
i get it for passionate, human made sites! but for actual, professional taking back the web sites, its always barebones css that
looks nice but feels lazy.
you also cant stick with web 1.0 for the simple fact that not everyone is old enough to get a feeling of nostalgia from web 1.0.
some people dont even remember what web 2.0 was like!
and that includes me. i think web 1.0 looks, frankly, unprofessional for the movement.

while i dont want to see people harassed into changing their website layout or something, i dont just want a real, human made web.
i want both that and a visually appealing web. a web where you can see peoples passions layed out in a smooth,
flowing matter, full of colours and expressions they like, yet with a soft feel where you can tell it isnt trying to be all corporate.

td:lr:
i want the old web back. i want to *experience* the old web. but the movement will never be easy,
and i also want to see peoples passions painted beautifully on their own digital canvas.
